# MA103 — Self-Check Quiz Bank

Informal, ungraded. Use these to spot-check yourself at the end of each week — if you can answer confidently without looking anything up, you're on track.

## Week 1 — Probability Foundations

1. What does `P(A|B)` actually represent, in terms of narrowing a sample space?
2. What's the difference between `P(A|B)` and `P(B|A)`? Why does mixing them up matter?
3. What does it mean for two events to be independent, precisely?
4. In Bayes' theorem, what role does the "prior" play?

## Week 2 — Random Variables & Distributions

5. What's the difference between a PMF and a PDF, and why can a PDF's value exceed 1 while a PMF's can't?
6. What real-world process does a Poisson distribution typically model?
7. What assumption does a Binomial distribution require about repeated trials?
8. Why does the Normal distribution show up so frequently across so many unrelated situations?

## Week 3 — Expectation, Variance & Covariance

9. Is the expected value of a distribution always a value the variable can actually take? Give an example either way.
10. What does linearity of expectation let you do, even when variables aren't independent?
11. What does correlation actually measure, and what does it NOT tell you?
12. Give an example of two variables that are correlated but not causally related, and name a plausible confounder.

## Week 4 — Descriptive Statistics & CLT

13. When would the median be a better summary statistic than the mean?
14. What's the difference between the distribution of raw data and a "sampling distribution"?
15. What does the Central Limit Theorem actually guarantee?
16. Does CLT mean individual data points become more Normal-like with more data? Why or why not?

## Week 5 — Hypothesis Testing & Confidence Intervals

17. What does a p-value of 0.03 actually mean, precisely?
18. What's wrong with saying "there's a 95% chance the true value is in this confidence interval"?
19. What's the difference between a Type I error and a Type II error?
20. Why can a statistically significant result still be practically meaningless?

## Week 6 — MLE & Bayesian Thinking

21. In plain language, what is Maximum Likelihood Estimation trying to do?
22. Why is minimizing mean squared error mathematically equivalent to MLE under a Gaussian noise assumption?
23. What's the core philosophical difference between MLE and Bayesian estimation?
24. In the prior/likelihood/posterior framing, what does the "posterior" represent?

## Full-Course Gut Check

25. Given a real dataset and a claim like "Group A performs better than Group B," walk through — out loud, without notes — how you'd test that claim rigorously: what test you'd run, how you'd interpret the p-value and confidence interval correctly, and what you would and wouldn't be able to conclude. If you can do this comfortably, MA103 is done.
